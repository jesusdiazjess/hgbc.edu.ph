Admission Policy

The college subscribes to the OPN admission policy for admitting students. All freshman applicants who meet the general requirements may be admitted. However, they are expected to maintain the academic standards set by the school for retention in succeeding years. The school reserves the right to refuse admission to applicants for reasons like limitations on school facilities, poor academic performance, sustatining activities, religious or otherwise, contrary to the institutional mission, philosophy, and goals of the college, a lack of moral fitness, and the like.

Consistent with the objective of producing quality graduates, HGBaquiran College strictly screens all student applicants for admission. A student can only be admitted to HGBaquiran College if he or she possesses the following basic requirements:
1. HS Report Card and Forms 137 and 138-A
2. Certificate of good moral character
3. 3 pieces of 2x2 colored pictures
4. Birth Certificate (PSA-authenticated)
5. Police or NBI clearance may also be asked in special cases.
6. Certificate for a drug test

A student who has been enrolled in a post-secondary course in another school and who desires to transfer to HGBaquiran College shall be admitted after an interview with the guidance counselor, dean or any authorized school representative. The following documents are required when seeking admission:
1. Transcript of records or true copies of grades. If official transcript of records in unavailable initially, Certification of Final grades of all subjects taken from the School Term must be submitted but the enrollee is considered as temporarily enrolled. The Official Transcript of Records must be submitted two(2) weeks from the start of the classes for the student to be considered permanently enrolled.
2.    Honorable dismissal from the last school attended.
3.    Certificate of good moral character from the last school attended.
4.    4pcs 2x2 colored picture with blue background.
5.    Birth Certificate (PSA Certificate).

A student who has completed an academic degree from any institution of recognized standing may be enrolled as determined by the Dean. However, before a student is allowed to major in any discipline, the college Dean/program Coordinator concerned may prescribe additional general education and/or preparatory course for the major field.

Married students may be admitted for enrollment on condition that they meet all admission requirements of the school and submit a copy of their marriage contract.
